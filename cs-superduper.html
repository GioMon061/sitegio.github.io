description:

Responsive web design concept for tech company.

The goal of the website was to increase conversion, by looking more trustworthy and professional, while also looking techie and cool to attract key talent and close partnership deals.

My first step was checking out the client’s favourite designs, and what they had in common. I wrote down a few notes for each design (colors, styles, shapes, images or illustrations). This led me to select the color scheme, the typography, and the style.

Then I roughly sketched -with simple pencil and paper- the 3 designs for desktop, tablet, and mobile. I imported the sketches into Figma and designed the 3 wireframes. Finally, I designed the 3 comps.

I chose a geometric style because it seemed to be the client's "favorite" style. It also matched the overall "tech" vibe all the chosen designs had. I picked some geometric patterns I could customize, as I could not design them myself in this instance.

Similarly, I chose the color scheme (the client seemed to had a preference for blues and corals) and the typography (no design in the client’s mood board had a serif font, so I chose to use only one sans-serif font available in many, different weights).

I wanted the design to have colors and still looking very professional, since the company publishes its researches on academic publications. This is why I chose to not add any illustration, only some flat icon.

Finally, I imagined a design that could be easily coded simply with HTML5 and CSS3. Three comps have been realised for the project: desktop, tablet and mobile.
